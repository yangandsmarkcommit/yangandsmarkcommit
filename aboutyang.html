Biography

Yang was born in China, and emigrated with her parents to St. Louis, Missouri when she was 4 years old. After a few fun years near downtown, they moved to a house in the suburbs in a good school district. The American dream was well underway. Yang's family was part of the small but vibrant ethnic Chinese community in the St. Louis metropolitan area. Yang attended Chinese school every week, where she learned Chinese folk dances and played with other Chinese Americans struggling with their identities. In high school, Yang couldn't help annoying people with her hyperactive energy. She was a regular in the math competition circuit, where nerds gather and trade puns. In middle school, she was part of a math team that called themselves the JERKS (Junior Educated Radical Kids). Yang went to Boston for undergrad at MIT, where she found fantastically artistic, eccentric, and thoughful people. She became interested in sustainability, and put together many events about the topic. After working in the power electronics industry for a couple years, she went to graduate school at UC Berkeley for a joint masters' degree in Public Policy and the Energy and Resources Group. Now she lives in Oakland and works in the responsible investing industry. She enjoys sharing meals, going on hikes, discussing politics, and hobbying with friends.
